What we've ruled out (and why)

A lab is only honest if it remembers its dead ends. Each of these was a reasonable idea that lost once tested out-of-sample. Knowing what does not work protects the account as much as the one edge that does — and stops us re-exploring the same holes.

Idea testedWhy it seemed good What the data said
US-market-hours gatingoff-hours prices look thinMade it worse (−1.8% vs +1.5%). Off-hours dips are fine on liquid names — our edge actually lives off-hours. Trade 24/7.
0% maker-fee entriesfree fee saving during the promoLoses — misses ~26% of trades (the quick-reversal winners). Even a maker→taker hybrid loses once the fallback is modelled honestly. Immediate taker entry is optimal.
Wider take-profit (3–4%)let winners run furtherLoses — more trades reverse before reaching the far target. The tight 2.2% TP banks the bounce before it fades.
Trailing-stop exitsride the big movesLoses (−3 to −8%). Trailing suits trend systems; ours is mean-reversion — bounces are quick and don't trend, so a fixed TP wins.
Momentum / trend-followingnew & volatile — gaps should continueLoses across the board (profit-factor < 1) on EMA-cross, Donchian and breakout, every timeframe. bStocks mean-revert in this regime, so trend strategies chop and bleed.
A 2nd mean-reversion edgeanother dip lens might add tradesNone survive. Bollinger / deviation / deep-RSI lose in-sample. “4 down-bars” looked great (+24%) but was overfit — only N=4 works and the gain was almost all one name. The RSI dip-buyer already captures the edge; the rest is noise.
Market-structure study (28 days of real bStock data) — this is why the surviving edge is shaped the way it is:
Off-hours & weekends mean-revert (1h autocorrelation −0.14 / −0.09) — dips bounce. This is why 24/7 dip-buying beats US-hours-only: the edge lives when the real market is closed.
The US session trends (autocorr +0.10) — during real trading hours a dip tends to keep falling, so we don't chase it there.
The US open (9–10am ET) is chaos (−0.30% avg move, 2.5% range) — but the quality gate already avoids the bad setups; explicitly skipping the open hour did NOT improve returns on holdout.
The pros' play is arbitrage (token price vs the real NYSE/Nasdaq price). That needs a live underlying feed + market-maker rails — a different architecture. Noted as a future direction, not a current miss.

What bStocks are (learned from Binance)

What they are
Tokenized US stocks — certificates, NOT direct share ownership. Issued by BTech Holdings (a Binance affiliate).
Backing
Each token 1:1 backed by a real share held with a regulated custodian. A Proof-of-Collateral page is published.
Chain
Standard BEP-20 tokens on BNB Chain — self-custody + DeFi compatible. Trade via the same /api/v3 spot endpoints as crypto.
Trading hours
24/7, weekends included — but the REAL US market is open only ~6.5h/day (09:30–16:00 New York). Off-hours prices go thin/gappy.
Fees
Zero conversion fee stock↔token; zero maker fee promo at launch; standard spot fees on secondary trades. Spreads still eat thin targets.
Minimum
Fractional from as little as $5 per trade.
Dividends / splits
Dividends auto-reinvest on the ex-date; splits & corporate actions processed automatically.
Launch set
Started with CRCLB, NVDAB, TSLAB, MUB, SNDKB, SPCXB. The traded universe now AUTO-GROWS: a name joins after 3 straight days clearing $800k/24h & ≤0.15% spread. Binance keeps listing more (10 added Jul 7 '26: GOOGL, QCOM, COIN, SPY, SOXL, DRAM, WDC, GLW, NBIS, CBRS).
Eligibility
ADGM (Abu Dhabi) only. NOT offered to US persons.
Age / risk
Listed ~11 Jun 2026 — about 6 weeks of history so far, still essentially one market regime. Any early result is directional, not proof.

Where we are & what's next

Built and running. The paper pipeline is live — 24/7 engine, daily research cycle, ML win-model, an auto-growing universe, and the full safety layer (earnings blackout, split/corporate-action guard, anomaly blackout). All forward-only, zero capital at risk.
Proving it now. The live paper account is accumulating a real out-of-sample record. The bar to clear: hold above break-even win-rate over ~200 trades — not the ~60 we have — so the result stops being luck.
Survive a second regime. Every result so far is one market mood (a mostly-flat/choppy tape). The edge must still work through a real up-trend and a real sell-off before it's trustworthy.
Then, and only then: let the ML model filter marginal entries, scale the proven edge across more liquid bStocks, and — as a separate, keys-required, manually-approved decision — consider real money.
